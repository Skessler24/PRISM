Section 1: Platform Overview
The SPED Command Center is a fully integrated, HIPAA and FERPA compliant platform built exclusively for special education teams. It operates as a living hive mind: every piece of data entered once is used everywhere — across all tabs, calendars, student tiles, logs, and reports. The platform serves both the full SPED team and individual providers, and is always updated to reflect new changes in district, state, and federal policies and regulations.
Core Principles
Principle
Description

Enter Once, Use Everywhere
Every student data point entered once flows automatically to all connected tabs, logs, calendars, and reports.
Student Tile as Source of Truth
Every form, template, and material is stored in the student tile folder — nothing is siloed.

Everything is printable and downloadable as a PDF at any time.

AI Is Assistive, Not Authoritative
AI assists and drafts; all AI-generated output is reviewed by a human professional before use or distribution.
Auto-Updating Policy Banners
Mandate and regulation banners update automatically when laws or district policies change.

Section 3: Navigation Design
The navigation system is designed for speed, clarity, and accessibility across the entire platform.
Top Navigation Bar: Displays the first 5 tabs by name, always visible.
Hamburger Menu (☰): Located on the right side of the top bar; reveals the remaining tabs in a slide-out drawer.
Active Tab Highlight: The currently active tab is always visually highlighted.
Breadcrumb Trails: Appear on all sub-pages so providers always know where they are.
Universal Access: All 8 tabs are accessible from any location within the app — no dead ends.
💡 DESIGN NOTE
The navigation structure is intentionally minimal so that even providers who rarely use the platform can navigate it without training. The hamburger drawer keeps the interface clean while keeping every tab one click away.
Section 4: Build Phases
The SPED Command Center is built in 10 sequential phases, each delivering a fully functional layer of the platform. Phases build on one another, with Phase 1 establishing the data backbone that all subsequent phases depend on.

All 8 tabs are built simultaneously as working skeletons. Each tab will contain correct section headers, layout, placeholder widgets, empty states with descriptive labels, and proper routing between tabs. The student data structure — the backbone that everything else feeds from — is fully established in this phase.

Full routing and navigation between all tabs confirmed working
Student data schema fully designed and implemented — every field, every relationship
OneDrive and Google Drive dual-storage connections established
HIPAA/FERPA compliance framework in place before any student data is accepted

✅ AI Task Completion Workflow
When the AI completes any action (e.g., "Send Notice of Meeting to parents of S.Ka."), the following steps occur automatically and in sequence:
Sends the document via encrypted email OR generates a PDF for the case manager
Notifies the assigned provider within the platform
Logs the action to the parent contact log in the student tile
Updates the Evaluation Tracker checklist
Updates the SPED Calendar with the event
Updates the Main Dashboard to reflect the completed action
⚠ AI REVIEW REQUIREMENT
All AI-generated output — including drafts, summaries, communications, and reports — must be reviewed and approved by a qualified human professional before distribution or use. The AI assists; the professional decides.

Section 6: Compliance and Security Notes
🔒 COMPLIANCE IS NON-NEGOTIABLE
HIPAA and FERPA compliance is built into every feature of the SPED Command Center — not added as an afterthought. There are no features, workarounds, or shortcuts that bypass compliance requirements.
Compliance Area
Implementation
HIPAA & FERPA Compliance
Non-negotiable across every feature; policy banners auto-update when district, state, or federal regulations change.

Encrypted email for all sensitive document delivery: NOMs, Consent for Evaluation, and all parent communications.

PandaDocs integration for signature collection on all required documents.

Section 8: Glossary
The following terms are used throughout this document and throughout the SPED Command Center platform.
Term
Definition
NOM
Notice of Meeting — the IEP meeting notification sent to parents/guardians prior to any IEP meeting.
SOAP Notes
Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan — the standard documentation format for each therapy or service session.
BIP
Behavior Intervention Plan — a written plan that identifies target behaviors and outlines strategies for behavior support.
FBA
Functional Behavior Assessment — an evaluation process to determine the cause or function of a student's challenging behavior.
MTSS
Multi-Tiered System of Supports — a framework of evidence-based practices provided across tiers of increasing intensity.
RTI
Response to Intervention — a specific MTSS approach focused on academic and behavioral progress monitoring and tiered intervention.
IEP
Individualized Education Program — the legally binding document that outlines special education services, goals, and accommodations for an eligible student.
AAC
Augmentative and Alternative Communication — tools and strategies that supplement or replace natural speech and writing.
LAMP
Language Acquisition through Motor Planning — a research-based AAC communication system using consistent motor patterns to build language.
SLP
Speech-Language Pathologist — a licensed specialist who evaluates and treats communication, language, and swallowing disorders.
OT
Occupational Therapist — a licensed specialist who addresses fine motor, sensory processing, and daily living skill needs.
FERPA
Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act — the federal law that protects the privacy of student education records.
HIPAA
Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act — the federal law that protects sensitive health information from being disclosed without patient consent.
